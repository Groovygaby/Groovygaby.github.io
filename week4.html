Fake News and the Real Life Consequences

I want to create a website about the impact of Fake News and its real-life consequences. Essentially, I envision a website organized into sections, like the format of the 7 Deadly Sins website we discussed in Week 3. I want my website to consist of extensive information with regards to the most notorious fake news stories out there, as well as tips on how to spot fake news.

Week Four Tasks: Design Considerations

  • I want to make my background colour a light grey with a contrast of red headings throughout the website. I got inspired to do this by the 7 Deadly Sins Website as well as the BBC News Website. With my topic, I believe sticking to a consistent colour scheme is best as I don’t want my design considerations to take away from the important content.

  • I want to have an image as a banner on my website so that there is something interesting that catches the eye, ideally it will be news-related. I will also want to have a block of text in the banner for each week that describes what I aim to achieve with each week’s task.

  • My font family will need to be clearly legible and I want all headings to have styling on them so that they stand out from the rest of the content. I would like it if my text is aligned and all of the sentences are justified as this will make it look neat and appealing.

  • I would like to use tables and lists to compile some of my information so that it gives it a nice formatted look, it will also serve to break up the paragraph text which can become repetitive to the eye.

  • I also want to ensure that my design considerations favour accessibility throughout the website, this is something that we will explore in the coming weeks.

  • I would like to include images, audio and video on my website to create further engagement with the content. Preferably I would create my own audio file so that I can have it relate directly to my audience, this could be displayed on the homepage as a fun add-on.

  • I will continue to test the user experience of my website as I build it and will reach out to friends who are within my target audience to test the website and give their opinions on its navigation and accessibility.
